Why You're Stuck

Why Your Amazon Organic Growth Has Plateaued

If your rankings have stalled, here are the five patterns behind it — and what we do differently.

01

Your SEO and advertising are working against each other

Most accounts mix Brand, Competitor and Generic keywords in one campaign. Spend flows to the highest volume keywords and the terms that build organic rank never get funded.

We manage both together — organic data feeds ad targeting, and ad sales velocity accelerates organic rank.
02

Listings are only optimized for keywords, not conversion

Amazon's algorithms — A10 and Alexa for Shopping — read more than keywords. They track sales velocity and surface products based on attribute completeness. Most listings miss the full signal stack.

We write listings that give the algorithm as many positive signals as possible.
03

Your backend, A+ Content, Brand Story and storefront are leaving organic rank on the table

Backend search terms are the only element here that directly influence indexing. But A+ Content, Brand Story, and storefront architecture all drive conversion rate and session duration.

We cover the full stack — built to convert the traffic you're already paying to send.
04

Large catalogues get inconsistent treatment

Manual work doesn't scale. A brand with 500+ SKUs ends up with hero listings optimised and everything else quietly losing rank.

Our automation tools apply systematic optimization across thousands of SKUs — proven on 18,000+ for Puma.
05

Listings aren't built for AI-driven discovery

Alexa for Shopping — Amazon's default AI shopping assistant — doesn't match keywords. It interprets intent. If your listing is missing complete attributes, use cases, and contextual clarity, it won't surface your product.

We build attribute completeness into every listing so your products show up in both traditional search and AI-driven discovery.

FAQ

Common Questions About Amazon SEO

What is an Amazon SEO service?

+

An Amazon SEO service optimises product listings to rank higher in Amazon search results, driving organic traffic and increasing sales. Unlike Google SEO, Amazon's A9 and A10 algorithms rank products based on purchase likelihood — making conversion rate, sales velocity, and listing relevance the primary ranking signals.

How is Amazon SEO different from Google SEO?

+

Amazon SEO focuses on product rankings within Amazon's marketplace, where conversions and sales velocity directly influence rankings. Unlike Google SEO, Amazon prioritizes buyer intent and purchase behavior over informational content or backlinks.

What does your Amazon SEO service include?

+

Keyword research, listing optimization, backend keyword strategy, A+ content recommendations, competitor analysis, and ongoing performance tracking to improve rankings and conversions.

How long does it take to see results from Amazon SEO?

+

Initial ranking improvements are typically visible within 2–4 weeks of optimization. Significant revenue impact usually follows within 4–8 weeks as listings gain traction and sales velocity improves.

What does Amazon Rufus / Alexa for Shopping mean for SEO in 2026?

+

Rufus — now rebranded as Alexa for Shopping as of May 2026 — is Amazon's generative AI shopping assistant. We build AI-readiness into every listing we optimise, so your products show up in both traditional search results and AI-generated recommendations.

Does Amazon Brand Registry affect SEO rankings?

+

Yes — significantly. Brand Registry unlocks A+ Content, Brand Story, Brand Stores, Sponsored Brands, Vine reviews, and Brand Analytics. These tools directly improve conversion rate and listing quality, which are the primary signals Amazon's algorithm uses to rank products.

How much does an Amazon SEO service cost?

+

Most brands working with experienced Amazon SEO agencies spend between $1,500 and $10,000 per month depending on catalogue size, number of marketplaces, and scope of work. At Growisto, pricing is scoped to what your specific catalogue needs.
